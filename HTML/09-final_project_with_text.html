Formulae basket

Area or a circle

The area of a circle is the amount of space inside the circular boundary. It is calculated using the formula:
A = πr²

Pythagoras’ Theorem

Pythagoras’ theorem applies to a right-angled triangle and is written as:
a² + b² = c²
Here, a and b are the two shorter sides, and c is the hypotenuse (the longest side opposite the right angle).

Equation of Motion

The formula
s = ut + 1/2at²
describes the displacement of an object moving with constant acceleration.The equation shows that distance depends on how fast the object started and how its speed changes over time due to acceleration.

Mass–Energy Equivalence

The equation
E = mc²
comes from Einstein’s theory of relativity. It states that mass and energy are interchangeable. Because the speed of light squared is extremely large, even a small amount of mass can be converted into a huge amount of energy.
